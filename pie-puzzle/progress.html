Sprint Progress

Sprint 1

We initially planned on having 3 puzzles in our box. Our goal for sprint two was to get a protoype of each puzzle working individually so we could work on integration in Sprint 2. Our first puzzle is a clock. When user moves the hands to the correct time (3:14), a servo will spin the clock out of the way to reveal the next puzzle. For Sprint 1, we were able to get a working script to turn the servo when a button was pressed. We planned to use a reed switch in the final version, but used a button as a place holder while waiting for it to be shipped. Since the puzzle on its own is very top heavy, we planned to create supports for the servo and clock face so everything would stay upright.

For our second puzzle, we originally planned to have a maze. This maze would have a pie-shaped peg the user would have to drag to certain locations inside the maze, when it got to each location a strip of copper tape on the moveable piece would complete a circuit. After the peg hits each location in the correct order, the user would be able to move on to the next puzzle. In sprint 1, one of the incomplete circuits was made and it was tested with the copper tape to make sure our arduino script would detect whether or not the circuit was open or closed.

For our third and final puzzle, we planned to have a key board of lettered buttons. When the user holds the "P", "I", and "E" buttons at the same time, a solenoid will push a small box upward. This box will contain the user's prize for completing our puzzle box.
