Sprint 3

The chassis for the portal turret had to have 3 main functions:

  • Look like the portal turret (including the oval body and 3 legs)
  • House all electrical components within the shell
  • Not fail under stress!
With this information in mind, we decided that the chassis itself would need to be laser-cut with 1/8" wood.
The main body (the oval) houses 3 separate shelves.
  • The bottom shelf houses the Raspberry Pi, central breadboard, and speaker system.
  • The middle shelf holds the rack-and-pinion system and infrared sensor.
  • The top shelf mostly serves its purpose as a means to hold the top curve of the oval together.
To hold up the body, there are 3 legs made of 1/4" wood supported by 3D printed feet with rubber bottoms. Beneath the bottom shelf of the chassis is a 1/4" base, as well.

Final Stretch - Demo Day!

One of the biggest challenges for the Mechanical Team was determining what material the shell would need to be made of. The shell itself would need to be able to

  1. Keep the aesthetic look of the portal turret.
  2. Be lightweight as not to affect the durability of the chassis and legs.
The Team initially decided on using foam due to its lightweight nature. However, it proved to be difficult to fabricate and thus difficult to alter in the case of the turret needing to be reshaped. Additionally, it caused a lot of bending stress on the racks, creating an issue of the gear stopping and not being able to bring the wings back in.
It was thus that the team quickly shifted over to using mesh and fabric to create the shell. It was a lot more moldable, easier to attach to the servos, and much more lightweight, creating less stress on the rack-and-pinion system. To cover the chassis, we used (admittedly funky) blue and orange fabric, as it was fairly light.
