Electrical

The electrical work and features in the Portal Turret replica.

The circuit contains various sensors to respond to external stimulus and motors to control movement.

Circuit Diagram

The circuit contains the following components: an accelerometer/magnetometer, an IR receiver, a sound sensing module, a PCF8591 ADC/DAC module, a 9V DC motor, a L293D motor driver, two 9g SG90 servos, and a button. Additionally, a speaker is connected to the Raspberry Pi via Bluetooth so audio can be played, and an IR remote control can be used to send IR codes to the IR receiver.


Accelerometer

The accelerometer/magnetometer used is the LSM303AGR 3-axis accelerometer and 3-axis magnetometer. This measures the acceleration forces and magnetic field strength in three dimensions. In this case, it is only being used to measure the acceleration of the Portal Turret so it can detect when is picked up or moved around.
The LSM303AGR is connected to a 3.3V source, ground, SDA1 I2C, and SCL2 I2C.


IR Receiver

The IR receiver can receive signals from an IR remote control. When buttons are pressed on the remote, an IR code is transmitted, which the IR receiver reads and sends to the Raspberry Pi. From there, the code is processed to trigger some sort of output response.
The IR receiver is connected to a 3.3V power source, ground, and GPIO17.


Sound Sensing

To read the intensity of nearby sounds, two components are necessary: the sound sensing module and the PCF8951 ADC/DAC Module. The sound sensing module outputs analog values, with a lower number corresponding to a louder noise. However, the Raspberry Pi 4 Model B does not built-in analog capabilities, making it unable to directly read the values outpuuted by the sound sensor. The PCF8951 ADC/DAC helps address this limitation, as it converts the analog signal the sound sensor outputs into a digital one that the Raspberry Pi can read. Both of these components come from Sunfounder.
The sound sensor is connected to a 3.3V source, ground, and the PCF8591 ADC/DAC while the PCF8591 is connected to the sound sensor, a 3.3V source, ground, SDA1 I2C, and SCL1 I2C. The `SIG` pin of the sound sensor is fed into the `AIN3` pin of the PCF8591 ADC/DAC.
