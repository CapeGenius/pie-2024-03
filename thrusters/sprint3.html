Sprint 3 Overview

In Sprint 3 of our drone development project, we successfully completed the electrical and firmware integration of the gas thruster reaction control system. This involved mapping out a diagram of connection placements on the proto board, soldering down electrical components, harnessing wires around the mechanical system. We also finalized the nozzle design, selecting the one capable of the highest thrust and mounted the 2 liter bottle air tanks.

Completed assembly of the gas thruster reaction control system on the drone

To ensure the functionality of our electronics, we used the working firmware from our seesaw to test. Good thing we did so, because we discovered that at higher pressures in the tank, we needed to supply more current in order for the solenoid valves to open. This testing also revealed that our ground row was floating due to a weak connection. Also during this testing, we witnessed that we'd need to lower the thrust commands in our firmware because it was so powerful!

Test revealing very powerful thrust

Finally, we implemented the adaptive 4 thruster firmware, tuning the PID parameters to until we achieved smoother balancing, by moving from a thrust application solely based on angle to one that also factors in velocity.

4 thruster stabilization in action

We are very excited to have finished designing our four thruster, self-stabilizing drone.
