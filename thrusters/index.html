Project Overview

Ghost Thrusters is a PIE project aimed at developing a self-stabilizing drone using a reaction-control system.

The goal of this project is to create a drone capable of maintaining its equilibrium during flight through the use of thrusters. Initially, we contemplated a single thruster design for stabilization. However, we ultimately adopted a two-thruster seesaw configuration to simplify our approach and ensure effective balance.

Throughout the development process, we have successfully integrated an Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) into our prototype. This integration allows for real-time monitoring of the drone's orientation, providing crucial data for achieving dynamic balance. Our testing phase has demonstrated significant progress, showcasing the ability of our drone to adjust thrust based on angular displacement effectively.

We successfully implemented a system made up of mechanical, electrical, firmware, and software components to reach our goal.
